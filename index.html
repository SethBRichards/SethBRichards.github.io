About

Hello, I’m Seth! I was born and raised in Portland, OR until I was 16, and then my family moved to Lahaina, HI. I currently live in Seattle, WA, where I’m pursuing my BS in Electrical Engineering at the University of Washington (Expected Graduation: June 2021).

When I moved to Hawaii, I joined my new high school’s FRC Robotics team, which exposed me to programming and circuitry for the first time. I was absolutely enthralled at first sight, leading me to take some Engineering design classes that taught me CAD, and gave me some hands-on experience with Arduino projects. A couple years later, I made up my mind and decided that Electrical Engineering was the best way for me to chase after my passions.

When I got to college, I joined the Orsborn Lab, a research lab at the university associated with the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering, where I currently volunteer. In the lab, I’m using MATLAB and Python to translate some legacy code for future use. I’m learning a lot about signal processing and analyzing techniques, which will be used to Brain-Computer Interface technologies later on. It’s cool stuff, and it’s helping me learn a lot about coding, academia, and teamwork.

My current plan is to focus my EE degree in the concentration my school refers to as “Power Electronics and Drives”. I just really like playing with circuits, math, and programming, and I see this path as the optimal one to do all three. I’m going to diversify the classes I take, though, so if you’re interested, check out my courses page.

Outside of school and work, I like to read science fiction, play racquetball, and keep up to date on global affairs. I used to be a competitive fencer; ask me about it if you’re interested!
